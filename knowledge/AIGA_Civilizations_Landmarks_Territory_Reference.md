## CIVILIZATIONS

Civilizations are chosen at account creation or can be switched in-game. Each provides permanent passive stat bonuses and a unique special troop type that replaces the standard version of that unit.

### Key strategic implication for AIGA
Civilization choice provides permanent bonuses to specific troop types and production. Players should align their civilization with their primary march troop type for maximum synergy. **This information was completely absent from AIGA before this document.**

---

### Chinese Civilization
**Passive Buffs:**
- Archers' Defense +2%
- Archers' Training Speed +2%
- Wood Production Speed +6%

**Special Troop:** Chu Ko Nu (replaces standard Archers)
- Swift-firing repeating crossbow
- Remarkable accuracy, rapid fire rate, extensive attack range
- Excels at defeating enemies across the battlefield

**Best for:** Archer-primary march players

---

### French Civilization
**Passive Buffs:**
- Swordsmen's Defense +2%
- Swordsmen's Training Speed +2%
- Food Production Speed +6%

**Special Troop:** Throwing Axemen (replaces standard Swordsmen)
- Launch throwing axes to disrupt enemy formations
- Greater attack range and enhanced damage vs ordinary swordsmen

**Best for:** Swordsmen-primary march players

---

### Byzantines Civilization
**Passive Buffs:**
- Cavalry's Defense +2%
- Cavalry's Training Speed +2%
- Gold Production Speed +6%

**Special Troop:** Cataphracts (replaces standard Cavalry)
- Exceptional reputation across East and West
- Formidable challenge to archers
- Considered the most formidable forces within a legion

**Best for:** Cavalry-primary march players

---

### Romans Civilization
**Passive Buffs:**
- Pikemen's Defense +2%
- Pikemen's Training Speed +2%
- Stone Production Speed +6%

**Special Troop:** Phalanxes (replaces standard Pikemen)
- Diverse formation strategies
- Execute flanking maneuvers from both sides
- Timely and adaptable combat orders — elite imperial warfare forces

**Best for:** Pikemen-primary march players

---

### Egyptians Civilization
**Passive Buffs:**
- Pikemen's Defense +5%
- Troop Load +5%
- Gathering Troop Unit Loss Prevention +5%

**Special Troop:** Chariot Archers
- Great power and long-ranged fighting ability
- Highly mobile chariots with hails of arrows
- Especially effective when enemies are fleeing or shields are destroyed

**Best for:** Gathering-focused players or Pike players who want stronger defensive bonuses. The +5% troop load bonus benefits all gathering marches.

**Note:** Egyptians give +5% to Pikemen Defense (vs +2% for Romans) but trade the Roman training speed bonus for gathering-focused bonuses. Better for players prioritising resource economy over training efficiency.

---

### British Civilization
**Passive Buffs:**
- Archers' Defense +5%
- Player Citadel's Defense Bonus +10%
- Production of Mills +5%

**Special Troop:** Longbowmen (replaces standard Archers)
- Stand in arrays firing continuously to suppress enemies
- Excellent archery and tactics
- Formidable in medieval wars

**Best for:** Archer-primary players who prioritise citadel defense and food production. Citadel Defense +10% is the highest defensive citadel bonus of any civilization.

---

### Japanese Civilization
**Passive Buffs:**
- Swordsmen's Defense +5%
- Attack Bonus Against Player Citadels +5%
- Attack Against Tribes in the World +5%

**Special Troop:** Wandering Swordsmen (replaces standard Swordsmen)
- Samurai spirit — honor, loyalty, varied fighting styles
- More mysterious and harder to predict

**Best for:** Aggressive Swordsmen players focused on PvP citadel attacks and tribe grinding. The +5% tribe attack bonus directly benefits daily stamina grinding for XP and event points.

**Note:** Miyamoto Musashi is Japanese civilization — natural synergy.

---

### Koreans Civilization
**Passive Buffs:**
- Cavalry's Defense +5%
- Looting Limit +500,000
- Resource Storage Protection Rate +10%

**Special Troop:** Korean Archers
- Move quickly to vantage points
- Fire on highlands or from concealed positions
- Accurate and frequent arrow hails that affect enemy morale

**Best for:** Cavalry players who also want looting and resource protection bonuses. Looting Limit +500K stacks with War Hall looting increases.

| Civilization | Primary Troop | Key Buff | Secondary Value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Chinese | Archer | Archer Def +2%, Training Speed +2% | Wood production +6% |
| French | Sword | Sword Def +2%, Training Speed +2% | Food production +6% |
| Byzantines | Cavalry | Cavalry Def +2%, Training Speed +2% | Gold production +6% |
| Romans | Pike | Pike Def +2%, Training Speed +2% | Stone production +6% |
| Egyptians | Pike | Pike Def +5%, Troop Load +5% | Gathering loss prevention +5% |
| British | Archer | Archer Def +5%, Citadel Def +10% | Mill production +5% |
| Japanese | Sword | Sword Def +5%, vs Citadels +5% | vs Tribes +5% |
| Koreans | Cavalry | Cavalry Def +5%, Looting +500K | Resource protection +10% |

**Pattern:** Chinese/French/Byzantines/Romans give +2% to their troop type with +2% training speed and +6% to a resource production. Egyptians/British/Japanese/Koreans give +5% to their troop type with more specialised secondary bonuses.

**AIGA advisory on civilization choice:**
- Match civilization to your primary M1 march troop type
- If swordsmen M1: French (+2%) or Japanese (+5% — better for PvP aggressive players)
- If cavalry M1: Byzantines (+2%) or Koreans (+5% — better for established players with looting focus)
- If archer M1: Chinese (+2%) or British (+5% — better for defensive players)
- If pikemen M1: Romans (+2%) or Egyptians (+5% — better if gathering is a priority)
- The +5% civilizations give stronger primary troop bonuses but with more niche secondary effects

## LANDMARKS & IMPERIAL CITY

### Imperial City
The server's ultimate prize. Controlling the Imperial City grants the victorious alliance leader the ability to appoint a **King**.

**Event structure:**
- Available on a regular basis once unlocked
- Clash duration: 4 hours
- Win condition: First alliance to occupy Imperial City for 1 hour, OR alliance with longest total occupation time

**Entry requirement:** Alliance territory must border the Imperial City to participate

**Sacred Towers:** Four towers surrounding the Imperial City — each grants a +20% attack and defense buff to the relevant troop type for troops inside the Imperial City during the event.

| Tower | Buff |
|---|---|
| Pike Sacred Tower | Pikemen attack and defense +20% in Imperial City |
| Sword Sacred Tower | Swordsmen attack and defense +20% in Imperial City |
| Cavalry Sacred Tower | Cavalry attack and defense +20% in Imperial City |
| Arrow Sacred Tower | Archers attack and defense +20% in Imperial City |

**Sun Legion:** During the event, Sun Legions spawn inside the Imperial City and actively attack all governors — an additional hazard beyond enemy alliances.

**Coronation:** Winning alliance leader appoints a King from their alliance. If no appointment is made before the time expires, the alliance leader is auto-coronated.

**AIGA note for Warlord tier:** Sacred Tower control is a key tactical objective. Capturing the tower matching your primary march troop type before entering the Imperial City gives a 20% attack/defense advantage. Alliance coordination on which towers to secure first is critical.

### City Clash Event
Weekly event where alliances compete to occupy cities across the world map.

**Rules:**
- Held weekly
- Each alliance can launch attacks on cities within each continent
- Win by occupying a city for the stipulated period — clash ends immediately on win
- If no alliance reaches minimum occupation time, last alliance to occupy wins
- Attacking a city does **not** require the city to be adjacent to your territory
- Leaving or disbanding the alliance is **prohibited** during the event

**War Declaration Process:**
1. Alliance leader or official uses war tokens to declare war on a city
2. War Preparation Period begins — troops cannot attack yet, use to prepare and rally allies
3. Siege Period begins when preparation ends — attack city center to reduce durability to 0
4. Occupation — last alliance to hit when durability reaches 0 claims the city
5. When multiple alliances attack simultaneously, the **last hit wins**

**Landmark Buffs (city occupation bonuses):**

| Buff Type | Example Cities |
|---|---|
| All Unit Types' Defense +5% | City of Shelter |
| All Unit Types' Health +5% | City of Honor |
| Might Damage Increase Rate +5% | City of Beauty |
| Strategy Damage Increase Rate +5% | Acropolis of Daybreak |

For Warlord-tier players coordinating alliance strategy, prioritising cities with damage buffs is higher value than health or defense buffs for offensive rally-focused alliances.

## TERRITORY

### Alliance Territory Structure

**Alliance Stronghold (Main)**
- Foundation of alliance territory
- Can be built anywhere accessible in the home region
- Must be connected — sub-strongholds connect to main or to each other

**Alliance Stronghold (Sub)**
- Built in the resource region
- Must be connected to main strongholds during construction
- After construction, connection is no longer required

**Alliance Flags**
- Expand alliance territory
- Must be built on land connected to strongholds or other flags
- Research alliance Science technology to unlock more flags
- Flags in resource region and Imperial City region cost more resources/funds but grant higher durability

**Alliance Gathering Center**
- Unlocked via alliance technology research
- Only 1 gathering center per alliance at any time
- Grants speed-up effect to alliance gathering
- Cannot be attacked during operation

### Territory Building Categories

Alliance territory buildings fall into 6 research categories, each with up to 7 levels:

| Category | Effect |
|---|---|
| Politics Building (0/7) | Alliance Politics Research Speed |
| Science Building (0/7) | Alliance Science Research Speed |
| Economy Building (0/7) | Alliance Economy Research Speed |
| Military Building (0/7) | Alliance Military Research Speed |
| Faith Building (0/7) | Alliance Faith Research Speed |
| Facility Building (0/7) | Alliance Facility Research Speed |
