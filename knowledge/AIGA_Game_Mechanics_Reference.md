Introduction to Beginners Guide
Age of Empires Mobile throws you into the heart of a sprawling empire, tasking you with building a thriving city, commanding legendary heroes, and conquering vast territories. Getting Started on Age of Empires Mobile
When starting Age of Empires Mobile, you will automatically be placed in a new kingdom. Choosing to play on a new server is important, as all players will be starting at the same time, giving you equal opportunity to engage in beginner events and milestones. On older servers, players have already significantly progressed, putting you at a disadvantage - so unless you are joining to play with friends, it is best you start on a new server (the one you automatically join will be the newest available server).
You will then need to choose one of eight available civilizations : Chinese, French, Byzantines, Romans, British, Egyptians, Japanese, or Koreans. We recommend in our civilizations guide that for the early game you should choose either the Chinese or Korean civilization. If this is your first Age of Empires Mobile server, you should probably pick the Chinese civilization. Don't stress your civilization too much though - its easy and cheap to change later!
City Building and Resource Management
The Town Center is the most important building in your city in Age of Empires Mobile. No other building can surpass the Town Center's level, acting as a cap for all other structures. We recommend rushing the Town Center to level 17 as quickly as possible. This is because you unlock another hero slot at this level, allowing you to field an army with three heroes, significantly boosting your attacking power.
As you progress and upgrade your town center, remember to keep resource management in mind. You gain resources over time from structures like farms and lumber mills. You can also acquire resources by killing barbarians, farming them in the world, or attacking other players. However, keep in mind that other players can also attack your city to plunder your resources.
Some key points of resource management:
Protection: The warehouse in your city provides protection for a certain amount of each resource, shielding them from plundering.
Resource Items: Avoid using resource items unless absolutely necessary for an upgrade. Using them unnecessarily can push your resources over the protected limit, making you a more attractive target for attackers.
Upgrading Warehouse: If you find yourself consistently overflowing with resources, consider upgrading your warehouse to increase the protected limit.

Embassy: This building allows you to receive Alliance Assistance, which can significantly speed up your building and research times. Upgrading the Embassy increases the number of assistance requests you can send - although more on alliances will come later in the guide.
University: The University is essential for research, and upgrading it provides additional research time bonuses. Research allows you to increase resource gathering speed, upgraded troops, and lots more.

What are Hero Medals?
Hero Medals are items that allow you to increase a hero's star level. You can obtain Hero Medals from events, shops, and packs. You can also obtain them from completing milestones in the game.
Upgrading your hero's star level is very important. Increasing your hero's star level will increase their might, which is the amount of damage a hero can do, and their armor, which will mitigate the amount of damage that hero will take. Each time you rank up your hero, you will also unlock more of their backstory.
You can see what skills your hero is capable of in the hero's menu. You will be able to see the hero's rage skill and signature skill. There will also be two customizable skills that you can change. When using heroes in battle, only the primary hero will use their rage skill. The other heroes in the march will use their signature skills and their customizable skills.
You will be able to increase the level of your hero's skills by using skill points. You can get skill points from daily events, from helping your alliance members, and from donating to Alliance technology. It is a good idea to save your skill points and not use them right away because it takes hundreds of thousands of skill points to max out a hero's skills. You can also use skill scrolls to upgrade your hero's skills.
When creating a troop, or march, it is important to use heroes that have the same military specialty and unit type. Each hero has a unit type, such as pikeman, swordsman, archer, or cavalry. Some heroes, like Harald III and Bushra, have two unit types. Each hero also has a military specialty, which is warrior, tactician, or marshall. If your heroes have the same military specialty, they will receive a 20% attribute bonus if two heroes share the same specialty, and a 30% attribute bonus if all three heroes share the same specialty.
If your heroes have the same unit type, they will get a 15% bonus on all attributes. However, this bonus is only activated if the heroes are leading troops that match their unit type specialty. For example, Joan of Arc specializes in the pikeman unit, so she would need to be leading pikemen to receive the attribute bonus. If you have three heroes in a troop that share the same military specialty and the same unit type, you will have a 45% attribute bonus.
When upgrading your heroes, you should prioritize upgrading heroes that are the most powerful that you have. It is also important to pick heroes that you have a lot of, so you will be able to increase their star level. Do not use Universal Legendary Hero Medals unless you are sure of the hero you are going to use.

Chinese Civilization Explained
The Chinese civilization in Age of Empires Mobile offers a 5% increase in Archers' Defense, a +8 villager capacity, and a +10% Walls Durability. The Archers' Defense is obviously only important if you have built a solid Archer march and is quite self explanatory. The villager capacity makes the Chinese a great early game choice because it allows you to gain resources faster, and have more villagers to use in the limited-time assignments. The +10% Walls Durability is pretty much pointless, as if you need it - it means you are being defeated anyway, so it is not something to hold value to.
French Civilization Explained
The French civilization in Age of Empires Mobile offers a 5% increase in Cavalry's Defense, a 5% in Resource Exchange Rate, and a 10% increase to Gathering Troops' Defense. Like the recommendation for the Chinese civilization, the 5% increase in Cavalry defense is really only a choice that you can make - if you have a strong cavalry march already and you feel like the buff here is worth it. The resource exchange rate is not too impactful because of how easy it is to acquire resource chests - that allow you to select what resource you get. The 10% increase to gathering troops' defense is very dependent on how your server is - if they are aggressive and 'tile hitting' (when someone attacks a resource tile) then it may be worth it, but if you do not encounter tile hitting then it would be a waste. From my experience, players tend to have a mutual agreement not to tile hit unless they are at war.
Byzantines Civilization Explained
The Byzantines civilization in Age of Empires Mobile offers a 5% increase in Swordsmen's Defense, a 5% increase in all unit types' siege damage, and a 5% increase in stone gathering speed in the world. Again the swordsmen's defense is very much up to yourself and your current troop composition. The unit types' siege damage in my opinion is not hugely worth it and there is better options available. The increase in stone gathering speed is always a welcomed addition, as it allows you to gain resources faster, meaning you can grow quicker.
Romans Civilization Explained
The Romains Age of Empires Mobile civilization offers an increase a Pikemen's Defense buff of 5%, as well as a 5% healing speed buff, and a +30 villagers' boost to buildings' production. Again, the pikemen's defense buff comes down to your troop composition. The healing speed buff may be nice in the later aspects of the game, but in the early-stage the healing times are already very short and so is not necessary. The villagers' boost to buildings' production is not hugely significant unless you are on an aggressive server that does not allow resource farming on tiles - although this may become more impactful as you gain more villagers, and could be helpful towards the mid to late game, but it is certainly not a viable early game choice.
