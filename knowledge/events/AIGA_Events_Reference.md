## Event Categories

AoEM events fall into five categories:
1. **Individual Events** — scored per player (MGE, MEE)
2. **Alliance Events** — alliance-wide participation
3. **Routine Events** — recurring scheduled events
4. **Bonus Events** — limited-time special events
5. **Store Events** — purchase-based progression systems

## Overview

Battle of Dawn is an alliance-versus-alliance (AvA) PvP game mode with a competitive ranking ladder. It is distinct from the Wonder Contest, though it shares some structural similarities in setup. The competitive ladder means consistent participation directly improves reward quality over time.

**Type:** Alliance PvP — competitive
**Frequency:** Approximately every 2 weeks
**Duration:** ~30 minutes per match *[verify in-game]*
**Entry:** Via the Event Center menu only — no Wonder required, no airdrops

---

## Setup and Registration

Registration works similarly to the Wonder Contest:

- Alliance declares intention to participate
- Officers form Legion teams from registered members
- Players select their class skill before entering (see Classes section below)
- Players configure two Legions before entering the match — each Legion is a march composition the player brings into the battlefield
- Legions can also be configured at the point of entry if not done in advance

---

## Map Layout

The map uses a sunflower design with circular paths and forest corridors.

- Both teams spawn at the **Hall of Faith** — one at the 12 o'clock position, one at 6 o'clock
- **6 temples** total — 3 on each side of the map (positioned at roughly 1, 3, 5 o'clock and 7, 9, 11 o'clock)
- Named temples include Moon Frost Temple and Gale Temple *[full temple name list — verify in-game]*
- **The Spire** sits at the center of the map — this is where the Relic spawns
- Forest corridors between dividing walls allow fast movement between map sections without crossing the center
- Players can flank around the outside of the map — you do not need to go through the center to reach enemy temples

---

## Core Objective

1. **Capture temples** — holding a temple earns score per second for your alliance
2. **Buffs from temples** — each controlled temple grants one of three buffs to your forces: all troop attack, all troop defense, or all troop health
3. **Relic delivery** — the Relic spawns at the Spire in the center after a fixed timer (~2 minutes from match start). Carry the Relic to any temple your alliance controls to earn additional points
4. **Monsters/bots** are also present on the map — defeating them provides resources and can serve as a tactical distraction

**Victory condition:** The alliance with the highest score at the end of the match wins and earns ladder points.

---

## Key Mechanics

- **No permanent troop loss** — dying in combat results in a fast respawn at your original spawn point (Hall of Faith). This keeps matches action-packed and means participation carries no account risk
- **Temple flipping** — temples can be recaptured at any time, even when your forces are elsewhere on the map. Map awareness is critical
- **Relic carrier** — whoever carries the Relic gains access to full map vision if playing as Monk class (see below). The Relic must be escorted to a controlled temple to score

## Classes

Players choose one class before entering. Class choice should be coordinated within the alliance to ensure a balanced composition.

| Class | Role | Ability |
|---|---|---|
| Ranger | Mobility / Strike | Charge out of forests for rapid engagement — effective for hit-and-run and flanking |
| Shadow Walker | Stealth / Ambush | Move unseen; stealth breaks on entering combat — ideal for surprise positioning |
| Centurion | Support / Escort | Boosts movement speed of nearby allies — best class for escorting the Relic carrier |
| Sacred Guardian | Tank | High durability — absorbs damage to protect teammates and hold contested points |
| Zealot | Sustain / Healer | Heals Legion troops after each kill — extends team's fighting time without retreating |
| Magistrate | Area Control | Deploys an AoE slow field — disrupts enemy movement and creates positional advantage |
| Monk | Vision / Intel | Grants full map vision when carrying the Relic — critical intel class for the Relic carrier role |
| Court Sage | Scout | Extended field of view compared to other classes — best for map awareness and intel gathering |

**Recommended synergies:**
- Zealot + Sacred Guardian: sustained front line that holds temple positions under pressure
- Centurion + Monk: fast, informed Relic delivery with movement speed support and full map vision
- Magistrate + Ranger: area denial slows enemies while Rangers charge in through forests

## AIGA Advisory Notes

- **No troop loss = no downside risk.** Every eligible member should participate regardless of account power level.
- **Gems are the standout personal reward.** They are consistently scarce and valuable — this event is a meaningful free source.
- **Ladder ranking compounds over time.** Alliances that participate consistently and climb tiers receive materially better rewards. Treat this as a long-term alliance investment, not a one-off event.
- **Class coordination is essential.** An alliance running eight Rangers will underperform versus one with a balanced composition. Officers should assign classes before the event starts.
- **Relic control wins matches.** Assign your fastest Centurion-supported group to Relic escort duty. Pair with a Monk for full map vision during delivery.
- **Temple awareness beats brute force.** Temples can flip while your main force is committed elsewhere. Always keep at least one march on temple watch.
- Event is most impactful at Governor tier and above — relevant for all active alliance members.

## Notes for Players

### What is Battle of Dawn?

Battle of Dawn is a team vs team battle event. Your alliance fights another alliance on a special map. The match lasts around 30 minutes and happens roughly every two weeks. There are no permanent troop losses — if you die in combat, you respawn quickly at your starting point and can jump straight back in. There is nothing to lose by participating.

### How do you win?

The team with the most points at the end wins. Points come from two things:

1. **Holding temples.** There are six temples spread across the map. Every temple your alliance controls earns points continuously while you hold it. The more temples you hold, the faster your score grows.
2. **Delivering the Relic.** About two minutes into the match, a Relic appears at the centre of the map. Whoever picks it up needs to carry it to a temple your alliance already controls. Successfully delivering the Relic earns bonus points.

Capture temples, hold them, deliver the Relic. That's the whole game.

### Instructions for players below T5

You are welcome and encouraged to participate. Your troops are weaker, but the mechanics favour smart play over raw power. Here is exactly what to do:

**Before the match:**
- Register when officers open sign-ups — do not skip this step
- Set up your two Legions (your two strongest marches) when prompted — officers may do this for you
- Pick your class when asked. If officers have not assigned you one, pick **Sacred Guardian** (tank) or **Zealot** (healer) — both are useful without needing strong troops
- If no guidance is given, Sacred Guardian is your safest default

**During the match:**
- Spawn at the Hall of Faith with your alliance — do not run off alone
- Move as a group. Stick with the larger cluster of your alliance
- Head toward the nearest temple on your side of the map first
- Stand in the temple to capture it — your presence counts even with lower-tier troops
- Once captured, stay near the temple to defend it. If an enemy approaches, fight them — even a small delay before you fall gives your teammates time to arrive
- If you die, you respawn immediately. Run straight back to where your team is
- Do not chase enemies across the map on your own
- Watch the Relic. If it appears at the centre and your team is heading for it, follow and help escort it back to a temple

**What to avoid:**
- Do not wander alone — you will lose every solo fight against stronger players
- Do not ignore a temple your team already holds to go fight somewhere flashy
- Do not stay at spawn after dying — respawn and get back in immediately

**The bottom line:** Your job is to be present, hold your ground on temples, and not die alone. Volume and coordination win this event, not individual power. Every body on a temple counts.

## What Is Wonder Contest?

Wonder Contest is a large-scale alliance PvP event where two alliances fight for control of Wonder Buildings across a dedicated battlefield map. It is considered one of the most fun PvP modes in the game. Crucially, **no troops die permanently** — there are no hospital bills. Troops auto-revive. Players can fight freely without resource risk.

Victory is determined by total points accumulated through occupation, barbarian kills, boss kills, and PvP eliminations. The alliance with the most points at the end of the match wins.

## Event Structure — 5 Days

| Day | Stage | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| Wednesday | Intention collection | Players indicate preferred participation times. Officers begin planning Legions. |
| Thursday | Sign-up | Officers create Legions (up to 5). Members sign up. Minimum 20 players per Legion required. |
| Friday | Matchmaking | Alliance matched against an opponent. |
| Saturday / Sunday | Battle | The actual Wonder Contest match is played. |

**Legion rules:**
- Up to 5 Legions per alliance, each at a different time slot
- Minimum 20 players per Legion — fall below 20 before gates open and the Legion is disqualified
- If a player leaves the alliance after signing up, their Legion spot is saved temporarily. They can return and still participate. If they do not return, they forfeit their slot and reduce the Legion count.
- Do not leave the alliance during Wonder Contest week unless absolutely necessary.

## How to Enter — Alliance Airdock

Entry requires the **Alliance Airdock**, an alliance building on the world map. Each player selects **up to 2 marches** to send into the contest. Once selected, those are the only marches that will participate. Send your two strongest combat marches.

Travel time to the Airdock is approximately 1 minute 40 seconds. A **6-minute preparation zone** follows on arrival before gates open and combat begins.

## Map Objectives — Priority Order

### 1. Fearless Monuments (PRIORITY FIRST)
Buildings that grant your marches attack buffs. On the Empire Arena this is +30% to all unit types. If both teams control their own Monuments, the gap is 60% in your favour versus an opponent who doesn't hold theirs. Capture these first on spawn before pushing toward Garrisons.

### 2. Wonder Outposts
Capture for minor Legion points and — critically — a **closer respawn point**. Troops who fall in combat revive at the nearest occupied Outpost. Use Outposts as forward staging areas when pushing Garrisons.

### 3. Wonder Garrisons
Major point structures. Multiple Garrisons on each map. Contested heavily mid-game. Occupation fills a progress bar — if troops from multiple legions are in the area, the bar pauses. Last alliance standing resumes filling it.

### 4. Wonder Center / Tower of Babel (unlocks at halfway point)
The highest-value structure. Unlocks in the center of the map at the halfway mark. The alliance that controls this in the final minutes earns the most points per minute. This is the decisive fight — be ready to contest it the moment it unlocks.

## Barbarians — Do Not Skip Them

Barbarians spawn everywhere on the map. Most players ignore them. This is a mistake.

- Killing barbarians earns personal points
- Barbarians drop **buff runes** on death — walk over a rune to collect it
- Collecting the same rune type **levels it up** (increasing its power)
- Teams that farm runes before engaging PvP will massively outperform teams that skip straight to fighting

**Practical rule:** Kill barbarians on the way to your objective. Do not make a detour, but never walk past one if it is in your path.

---

## Rune System

Runes are equipped to your march and provide combat buffs during the contest. You carry a limited number of rune slots. Picking up duplicate rune types levels them up rather than consuming another slot. You can voluntarily drop a rune to free a slot for a better one.

**Legendary Runes:** Only one can be equipped at a time. They are significantly more powerful than standard runes. Only dropped by Legendary Wonder Spirits (level 8 bosses).

## Legendary Wonder Spirits (Level 8 Bosses)

Massive boss units that spawn at set locations on each map. Confirmed to spawn on opposite sides of the map (e.g. North and South on Empire Arena, East and West on Hanging Gardens).

- Do not attack alone if you are not a top-tier player

**Tactical priority:** When the boss spawns, your alliance should coordinate a focused kill. Enemy teams will attempt to contest the kill or pick off your players while you fight the boss — maintain awareness.

## Tactical Notes from Live Play

**No hospital bills.** Fight without fear of permanent losses. This is the best mode in the game for testing and learning march performance.

**Troop tier disadvantage is survivable.** T5 vs T7 is playable — hero quality matters more than troop tier in this mode. A T5 Mulan can deal meaningful damage to T7 opponents.

**Hero performance observed:**
- Mulan: exceptional — high burst, excellent in 2v1 scenarios, strong against infantry
- Julius Caesar (pike): outstanding — does enormous work in the multi-player chaos of Wonder Contest
- Richard / Leo (pike): similarly strong in crowded fights
- Marshall Infantry builds: underperformed in live footage — not recommended as primary composition
- Solon: weak, melts quickly when focused

**Voice comms are decisive.** Teams with coordinated VC consistently outperform teams reacting individually. Assign a shot-caller before the match.

**Quality beats quantity.** A 21-player team with strategy beat a 30-player team in documented live gameplay. Do not assume more players equals a win.

**Drag targeting technique:** Select a target, begin combat, then drag your cursor to the next target before the first fight ends. When the first march finishes, your troops immediately engage the next target. This eliminates dead time between engagements.

**Battle reports not currently available** in Wonder Contest — you cannot review individual fight outcomes after the fact. *[verify whether this has been added in a later update]*

## Match Flow — Standard Game Plan

1. **Spawn at Wonder Airfield** (6-minute prep zone before gates open)
2. **Immediate priority:** push to Fearless Monuments and capture — this is your team's attack buff
3. **Push Outposts** — establish forward respawn points near contested Garrisons
4. **Contest Garrisons** — the team that holds more Garrisons mid-game builds the point lead
5. **Farm barbarians** throughout — every player on the map should be collecting runes passively
6. **Kill Legendary Spirits** when they spawn — coordinate as alliance, defend the kill
7. **Halfway mark — Wonder Center unlocks** — all available forces converge. This is the decisive fight. Losing it from a tied position is very difficult to recover from.
8. **Hold and defend** through the final minutes

## Rewards

**Per-match rewards:**
- Hero medals based on points scored × 2.25 multiplier
- Resources
- Victorious Legion additionally earns Wonder Blueprints and Flames (required for building Alliance Wonders)

**In-match store** (currency earned during play):
- Legendary hero medals
- Gear forging kits (recommended purchase — scarce and high value)
- XP tomes
- Extra summons

Store refreshes on a timer. Buy forging kits every refresh.

**Season rewards:** Based on end-of-season alliance ranking. Accumulate wins across matches to climb the seasonal leaderboard.

## Alliance Wonder Buildings (Unlocked by Winning)

Winning Wonder Contest grants access to **Alliance Wonder Buildings** — permanent structures your alliance builds on the world map. Each has its own tech tree and upgrades over time.

| Wonder | Effect |
|---|---|
| Empire Arena | Ally defence bonus +41% on adjacent flags |
| Tower of Babel | Ally attack bonus +10% on adjacent flags |
| Tornado Wonder | Releases tornadoes to Alliance lands for 10 minutes |
| Lightning Wonder | Releases lightning to specific land for 30 minutes |

Effects apply to **adjacent flags** — not just the tile the Wonder is built on. Placement matters.

**Strategic purpose:** Build and max out tech in your home Kingdom, then deploy these Wonders to strategic map locations during KvK / MEE for territorial combat advantage.

**Unknown:** Whether a maxed Wonder retains its tech if demolished and rebuilt elsewhere. *[verify in-game — believed to be the intended mechanic based on community observation]*

## AIGA Recommendations

- Send your two strongest combat marches — not your gathering march
- Equip offensive runes (Attack III, Damage III, Might/Strategy Damage based on hero type) — avoid stacking only defence runes unless you are designated anchor/tank
- Fearless Monuments first — always. A 30% attack gap in the first minutes shapes the whole match
- For non-PvP players: barbarian farming and Outpost occupation are valid contributions that earn personal points without requiring direct combat
- Best use of this mode: learn your march performance against real opposition with zero resource penalty. Use what you learn before MEE war stage.
