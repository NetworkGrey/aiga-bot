## Critical Mechanic — Talent Trees Stack

All three heroes in a march contribute their talent tree bonuses simultaneously. This means the third hero is never "wasted" on stats — their full talent tree applies even as second or third in command. Always level all march heroes to max to capture full talent tree value.

---

## How to Use This Document

Each composition lists heroes in march slot order: **Lead | Support 1 | Support 2**

The lead hero's commander skill fires first and most often. Supports contribute signature skills, secondary strike skills, and talent tree stats.

When a player uploads a spec sheet, match their available heroes against these compositions to identify the closest achievable march for each troop type. Flag gaps by name and tier.

**Source quality:** Community recommendations, not source-verified data. Treat as *[community knowledge]*. All compositions updated September 2025 on source site.

## Swordsmen Compositions

### Tier 1 — Meta Compositions

**Miyamoto Musashi + Yodit + Tribhuwana**
- Role: Attack/Active
- Why it works: Miyamoto signature has 13.41% chance to trigger another hero's signature — with Yodit and Tribhuwana both having high-activation signatures, this creates a chain reaction of skill triggers. Tribhuwana boosts Miyamoto's activation chance further via Ode to Java. Yodit's Deterrence stacks grow while Tribhuwana handles recovery and armor debuff.
- Troop: Swordsmen

**Sun Tzu + Theodora + Philip IV**
- Role: Strategy damage
- Why it works: Sun Tzu is the strategy damage lead. Theodora provides support and active skills. Philip boosts strategy damage via Strategy Master's Gift and High Spirit. All three have high strategy stats and charging/active synergy.
- Troop: Swordsmen

**King Arthur + Miyamoto Musashi + Constantine**
- Role: Attack/Active premium
- Why it works: King Arthur's Blessing of Glory ignores defense. Miyamoto triggers other heroes' skills. Constantine provides recovery and buff support. Requires King Arthur (VIP 17).
- Troop: Swordsmen (also works Cavalry)

**King Arthur + Mehmed + Lagertha**
- Role: Attack/Support premium
- Why it works: King Arthur leads with defense ignore. Mehmed provides dual damage reduction + active skill boost via Fatih. Lagertha adds defense penetration via Valkyrie's Edge and Raging Bloodline buff. Strong sustained output with damage mitigation.
- Troop: Swordsmen

**Yodit + Mehmed + Lagertha**
- Role: Attack/Support (non-Arthur alternative)
- Why it works: Yodit leads with Deterrence stacking. Same Mehmed + Lagertha support shell as above. Strong option when King Arthur is unavailable.
- Troop: Swordsmen

### Tier 2 — Solid Alternatives

**Josephine + Hammurabi + King Derrick**
- Role: Attack/Siege hybrid
- Why it works: Josephine has Siege Breaker for city attacks. Hammurabi brings Siege and Plunder. King Derrick buffs attack. Flexible swordsmen composition with siege capability.
- Troop: Swordsmen

**Cleopatra + Theodora + Philip IV**
- Role: Strategy/Support
- Why it works: Cleopatra is the strategy-leaning cavalry/swordsmen support. Theodora and Philip complete the strategy damage trio. Lower ceiling than Sun Tzu lead but more accessible.
- Troop: Swordsmen

**Oda Nobunaga + Toyotomi Hideyoshi + Tokugawa Ieyasu**
- Role: Turn-based/Japanese trio
- Why it works: All three are Marshal/swordsmen heroes with turn-based and active synergy. Strong thematic pairing with stacking talent trees. Mid-tier ceiling but solid for players with these heroes developed.
- Troop: Swordsmen

**Ramesses + Charlemagne + Zhuge Liang**
- Role: Strategy/Active
- Why it works: Ramesses activates signature extra times per second during Divine Protection. Charlemagne and Zhuge Liang provide support and strategy amplification. High activation rate composition.
- Troop: Swordsmen

## Pikemen Compositions

### Tier 1 — Meta Compositions

**Richard + Boudica + Frederick Barbarossa**
- Role: Counterattack/Defense
- Why it works: Richard and Boudica both have strong counterattack signatures and Forceful Retaliation/King's Blade. Frederick adds defensive recovery via Peaceful Haven and Tenacity. Three layers of counterattack mechanics running simultaneously. Best against normal-attack heavy opponents.
- Troop: Pikemen

**Richard + Boudica + Joan of Arc**
- Role: Counterattack/Recovery
- Why it works: Richard and Boudica provide the counter core. Joan of Arc adds Fearless Retribution (counter + recovery) and Righteous Judgement charge skill. Slightly more offensive than the Frederick variant.
- Troop: Pikemen

**Leonidas + Joan of Arc + Frederick Barbarossa**
- Role: Counterattack/Defense
- Why it works: Leonidas scales with unit loss — pairs well with Frederick's recovery keeping units alive long enough to use the damage bonus. Joan provides additional counter and recovery. Full defensive counterattack march.
- Troop: Pikemen

**Julius Caesar + Octavian + Bushra**
- Role: Turn-based/Support
- Why it works: Caesar and Octavian are both turn-based pikemen leads (Caesar has Change of Formation for turn-based boost, Octavian has Supreme Mark mechanic). Bushra provides recovery and support. Strong organised turn-based composition.
- Troop: Pikemen

**Julius Caesar + Octavian + Qin Shi Huang**
- Role: Turn-based
- Why it works: Alternative to the Bushra variant — Qin Shi Huang adds additional offensive turn-based layers. *[Qin Shi Huang hero guide not yet in AIGA reference — verify build]*
- Troop: Pikemen

**Ram Khamhaeng + Otto + Qin Shi Huang**
- Role: Buff/Turn-based
- Why it works: Ram Khamhaeng's Splendor of Sukhothai buffs all nearby allied commanders in rallies. Otto provides Holy Roman Empire turn-based damage. Qin Shi Huang adds more offensive pressure. Strong rally-support composition.
- Troop: Pikemen

### Tier 2 — Solid Alternatives

**Leonidas + Vlad + Mansa Musa**
- Role: Counterattack/Reactive
- Why it works: Leonidas counters on incoming attacks. Vlad adds Deadly Dragonthron counter mechanics (ignores defense every 4 triggers). Mansa Musa provides reactive recovery and armor debuff via Spend a Fortune.
- Troop: Pikemen

**Cyrus the Great + Vlad + Mansa Musa**
- Role: Counterattack/Reactive
- Why it works: Cyrus has Child of Prophecy (30% counter, every 4th guaranteed crit + reduces crit damage taken). Vlad adds a second counter layer. Mansa Musa rounds out the reactive triggers. Alternative to the Leonidas variant.
- Troop: Pikemen

**Belisarius + Elizabeth + Ashoka**
- Role: Strategy/Secondary Strike (Pikemen)
- Why it works: Belisarius leads with Conquering Iron Hoof strategy damage that stacks per activation. Elizabeth adds Triple Offensive and strategy support. Ashoka provides secondary strike skills and support.
- Troop: Pikemen

## Cavalry Compositions

### Tier 1 — Meta Compositions

**Lu Bu + Guan Yu + Attila the Hun**
- Role: Critical strike/Double attack
- Why it works: Lu Bu and Guan Yu both have Critical Insight (all heroes crit rate +5.13% each, stacking to +10.26%). Guan Yu's Berserker adds +32.32 might on crit. Attila puts all heroes into double attack. Lu Bu's Ripper Tiger builds crit rate further. Highest-crit cavalry composition in the game.
- Troop: Cavalry

**Lu Bu + King Arthur + Attila the Hun**
- Role: Critical/Active premium
- Why it works: King Arthur replaces Guan Yu for access to Blessing of Glory (defense ignore) + Spirited Pursuit high damage. Attila double attack support. Requires both King Arthur and Lu Bu — premium investment.
- Troop: Cavalry

**Lu Bu + Timur + Attila the Hun**
- Role: Critical/Defense ignore
- Why it works: Timur's Exotic Cavalry has 70% chance to ignore 17.88% defense. Lu Bu leads crit. Attila double attack. Similar ceiling to King Arthur variant but more accessible.
- Troop: Cavalry

**Hannibal + Justinian the Great + Ashoka**
- Role: Debuff/Recovery
- Why it works: Hannibal steals enemy armor and strategy via Peerless signature. Justinian provides constant recovery via Defensive Assault (triggers on every secondary strike). Ashoka adds secondary strike and support. Sustained cavalry composition with strong debuff pressure.
- Troop: Cavalry

**Hannibal + Justinian the Great + Cleopatra**
- Role: Debuff/Recovery/Gather hybrid
- Why it works: Same Hannibal + Justinian core. Cleopatra replaces Ashoka for gathering march flexibility — Cleopatra can be swapped to M5 without losing the core. Also adds strategy damage support to complement Hannibal's mixed damage.
- Troop: Cavalry

**Guan Yu + Attila the Hun + Darius the Great**
- Role: Critical/Gather hybrid
- Why it works: Guan Yu leads crit cavalry. Attila double attack. Darius adds piercing and gathering capability — useful for a player needing their cavalry march to double as a gathering march during peace. Note: Darius has gather specialty so gear/skills need to be combat-configured for war.
- Troop: Cavalry

### Tier 2 — Solid Alternatives

**Cid + Saladin + Robin Hood**
- Role: Rage/Support cavalry
- Why it works: Saladin generates rage passively and on skill activations. Cid is a secondary strike cavalry marshal. Robin Hood adds support. *[Robin Hood hero guide not yet in AIGA reference — verify build]*
- Troop: Cavalry

**Cid + Tomyris + Robin Hood**
- Role: Buff/Rage cavalry
- Why it works: Tomyris boosts other heroes' might damage and reduces damage taken. Cid leads. Robin Hood support. Tomyris signature synergy means the more skills Cid activates, the more damage reduction applies.
- Troop: Cavalry

**Belisarius + Hannibal + Ashoka**
- Role: Strategy/Secondary Strike cavalry
- Why it works: Belisarius leads with Conquering Iron Hoof (stacking strategy damage). Hannibal provides mixed debuff support. Ashoka adds secondary strike skills. Strategy-focused cavalry alternative.
- Troop: Cavalry

**Yi Seong Gye + Sejong the Great + Bushra**
- Role: Active/Defense cavalry
- Why it works: Yi Seong Gye is an active cavalry marshal. Sejong provides defense, recovery and Entrenchment. Bushra adds support and recovery. Defensive cavalry option. *[Yi Seong Gye and Sejong builds not yet in AIGA reference — verify]*
- Troop: Cavalry

## Archer Compositions

### Tier 1 — Meta Compositions

**Hua Mulan + Bellevue + Attila the Hun**
- Role: Attack/Double attack
- Why it works: Hua Mulan's Starchaser triggers 3-hit secondary strikes at 50% chance. Bellevue adds Pay in Blood (74.74% damage + recovery after normal attack) and Act of Mercy (scales with enemy unit loss). Attila puts all three heroes into double attack, doubling Hua Mulan's secondary strike frequency. Top-tier archer meta composition.
- Troop: Archer

**Suleiman + Queen Seondeok + Theodora**
- Role: Strategy damage/Debuff
- Why it works: Suleiman leads strategy damage with debuff-conditional bonus effects. Queen Seondeok debuffs enemy strategy damage taken (+5.96%) and silences. Theodora provides additional strategy support and active skills. Three strategy heroes with stacking debuffs — one of the strongest strategy compositions available.
- Troop: Archer

**Suleiman + Theodora + Yi Sun-Shin**
- Role: Strategy damage/Gather hybrid
- Why it works: Same Suleiman + Theodora core. Yi Sun-Shin replaces Queen Seondeok — slightly lower debuff ceiling but Yi Sun-Shin has Organized Retreat (survival during gathering) making this usable as a dual-purpose march during peace.
- Troop: Archer

**Bellevue + Timur + Mehmed**
- Role: Attack/Defense ignore/Support
- Why it works: Bellevue leads with Act of Mercy (scales damage with enemy unit loss) and Pay in Blood. Timur adds 70% defense-ignore on commander skill + opening burst buff. Mehmed provides dual damage reduction and active skill boost. Newer meta composition combining three recent additions to the roster.
- Troop: Archer

**Hua Mulan + Timur + Mehmed**
- Role: Attack/Defense ignore/Support
- Why it works: Hua Mulan leads secondary strike attacks. Timur adds defense ignore. Mehmed provides support. Alternative to Bellevue lead when Hua Mulan is more developed.
- Troop: Archer

### Tier 2 — Solid Alternatives

**Josephine + Rani Durgavati + Hua Mulan**
- Role: Attack/Double attack
- Why it works: Josephine leads with attack and recovery. Rani Durgavati provides Warrior's Hymn (might +25.25 + crit rate +4.04% to next hero) and double attack specialty. Hua Mulan as third in command still contributes full talent tree. More accessible composition for players without Bellevue or Timur.
- Troop: Archer

## Gathering March

**Diao Chan + Cleopatra + Darius the Great**
- Role: Gathering only — never use in combat
- Why it works: Diao Chan signature gives 1.5x daily resources regardless of hero level — irreplaceable gathering lead. Cleopatra adds Efficient Harvest (+14.35% gather speed). Darius adds gather speed + piercing talent tree. Highest resource yield per march in the game.
- Config: M5 during peace. Swap Darius to M3 combat on war switch.
- Note: Toyotomi Hideyoshi can replace Darius if Darius is needed in a combat march.

## March Slot Priority Framework

When advising a player on march configuration:

| March | Primary Role | Composition Priority |
|---|---|---|
| M1 | Main rally lead | Best available lead + 2 best supports for that troop type |
| M2 | Secondary rally | Second troop type — Pike has no hard counter weakness, safest choice |
| M3 | Tribe grinding (peace) / rally (war) | Third troop type or duplicate of M1 troop |
| M4 | Tribe grinding (peace) / rally (war) | Fourth troop type or flexible |
| M5 | Gathering (peace) / rally (war) | Diao Chan lead during peace; swap to combat for war |

**Troop type assignment guidance:**
- M1: Player's strongest troop type and hero investment
- M2: Pike is safest — no hard counter weakness
- M3: Cavalry or Archer depending on roster
- M4: Remaining troop type
- Never mix troop types within a march

1. Attila stays with Lu Bu. Mehmed II goes to Mulan march.
